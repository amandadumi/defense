Electronic Structure Problem:

describe the motions of electrons in atoms or molecule \[\hat{H}\left|\Psi\right>\ =\ E\left|\Psi\right>\]

Approximations:

\[\begin{align} \hat{H} &= \sum_{i}\left[ -\frac{1}{2}\nabla_i^2 -\sum_{iA}\frac{Z_A}{\left|\vec{r}_i-\vec{R}_A|\right|}\right] + \sum_{i<j}\frac{1}{\left|\vec{r}_i - \vec{r}_j\right|} \\ \hat{H} &= \hat{h}(1) + \hat{v}(1,2) \end{align}\]

Non-relativistic, time-independent

Born-Oppenheimer: nuclei are stationary

Single particle orbitals: integrals \[\hat{H} = \hat{h}(1) + \hat{v}(1,2)\]

\[\begin{align} \langle i \rvert \hat{h}(1) \lvert j \rangle\ &=\ \int d\textbf{x}_1 \chi_i(\textbf{x}_1)\hat{h}(1) \chi_j (\textbf{x}_1)\\ \left[ij \rvert kl\right]\ &=\ \int d\textbf{x}_1d\textbf{x}_2 \chi_i^*(\textbf{x}_1)\chi_j(\textbf{x}_1) \hat{v} \chi_k(\textbf{x}_2)^* \chi_l(\textbf{x}_2) \end{align}\]

Single particle orbitals: Slater Determinant \[\Psi=\frac{1}{\sqrt{N !}}\left|\begin{array}{cccc} \chi_{1}\left(\mathbf{x}_{1}\right) & \chi_{2}\left(\mathbf{x}_{1}\right) & \cdots & \chi_{N}\left(\mathbf{x}_{1}\right) \\ \chi_{1}\left(\mathbf{x}_{2}\right) & \chi_{2}\left(\mathbf{x}_{2}\right) & \cdots & \chi_{N}\left(\mathbf{x}_{2}\right) \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \chi_{1}\left(\mathbf{x}_{N}\right) & \chi_{2}\left(\mathbf{x}_{N}\right) & \cdots & \chi_{N}\left(\mathbf{x}_{N}\right) \end{array}\right| \]

Hartree-Fock: a mean field approach

Electrons feel the repulsive force of an average electron cloud.

\(\hookrightarrow\) Motion of individual electrons are not correlated

\(\hookrightarrow\) Finding the “best” orbitals for a single Slater determinant

\[ E_{HF} = \sum_i^{elec}\langle i \rvert h(1) \lvert i \rangle + \sum_{i>j}^{elec}\left[ ii \rvert jj\right] - \left[ ij \rvert ji\right]\]

Coulomb \(\left[ii \rvert jj\right]= \int d\textbf{x}_1d\textbf{x}_2 \color{red}{\chi_i^*}(\color{blue}{\textbf{x}_1})\color{red}{\chi_i}(\color{blue}{\textbf{x}_1}) \hat{v} \color{green}{\chi_j^*}(\color{orange}{\textbf{x}_2})\color{green}{\chi_j}(\color{orange}{\textbf{x}_2})\)

Exchange \(\left[ij \rvert ji\right]= \int d\textbf{x}_1d\textbf{x}_2 \color{red}{\chi_i^*}(\color{blue}{\textbf{x}_1})\color{green}{\chi_j}(\color{blue}{\textbf{x}_1}) \hat{v} \color{green}{\chi_j^*}(\color{orange}{\textbf{x}_2})\color{red}{\chi_i}(\color{orange}{\textbf{x}_2})\)
